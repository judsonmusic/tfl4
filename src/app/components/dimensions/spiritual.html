Definition of Spiritual:

"We are not defining spirituality as following a particular religion, although practicing a religion can help deepen your spirituality.  For the purpose of this dimension, we invite you to think about Spirituality as, ‘feeling a sense of peace and belonging, knowing that you are part of something greater than yourself." - Terrie Elizabeth Reeves, Founder, Empower Health America

"Spirituality is the aspect of humanity that refers to the way individuals seek and express meaning and purpose and the way they experience their connectedness to the moment, to self, to others, to nature, and to the significant or sacred." - Christina Puchalski, MD, Director, George Washington Institute for Spirituality and Health

TFL Self-Guided Exercises:

Take time to think about the following questions and perhaps answer them in a personal Ideal Being Journal that you can go back and reference and add to over time. We encourage you to share and discuss your answers with a coach, counselor or friend. You will be asked to answer the same seven questions for each dimension and we encourage you to take the time to do this self-reflective exercise. Your answers will give you greater clarity and a good foundation in which to begin your journey to a state of Ideal Being.

  1. What does your life currently look and feel like in this dimension?
  2. Specifically what are the things you are doing to feel satisfied in this dimension of your life?
  3. What do you envision your life to look and feel like if you gain greater satisfaction in this dimension?
  4. Specifically what would you like to integrate into your life in this dimension?
  5. What are the barriers that are keeping you from improving this dimension of your life?
  6. What can you do to eliminate or navigate around these barriers?
  7. What tools and support do you need to be successful?

Create a Sacred Space:

Pick a place in your home that you can designate as your sacred space and make it a comfortable and esthetically appealing place to spend time.  Get in the habit of spending 10 to 15 minutes a day in this area in prayer, meditation, contemplation or journaling.

Did You Know:

"About 95% of Americans recently professed a belief in God or a higher power, a figure that has never dropped below 90% during the past 50 years, and 9 out of 10 people also said that they pray, most of them (67%-75%) on a daily basis.  Many Americans have stated that their faith is a central guiding force in their lives.  Over two-thirds (69%) recently reported that they were members of a church or synagogue, and 40% reported that they attended regularly (1)."

"Spirituality gives expression to the being that is in us; it has to do with feelings, with the power that comes from within, with knowing our deepest selves and what is sacred to us, with, as Matthew Fox says, ‘heart-knowledge’ (2)."

TFL Actions to Consider:

  1. Read a daily devotional message.
  2. Practice meditation.
  3. Participate in individual or group prayer.
  4. Take part in community worship.
  5. Join a small group of like-minded people to grow, share and strengthen your values.
  6. Spend time in nature.
  7. Join a volunteer group that aligns with your core values.
  8. Give back or help someone in need. 
  9. Do a random act of kindness for a stranger and someone you love today.
  10. Follow a spiritual blog.
  11. Do yoga to slow down and to connect spiritually.
  12. Read books that uplift and align with your beliefs.
  13. Develop tolerance, patience and compassion for yourself and others.
  14. Remain alert and open-minded to the opportunities and miracles happening in your life. 
  15. Take time each day to quiet your mind and to be thankful.
  16. Look inside yourself and evaluate what it is that makes you feel alive and do more of it.
  17. Identify the good in your life and give thanks to God or your higher power.   
  18. Cultivate an attitude of gratitude and look for the lessons and blessings in all circumstances.
  19. Start a gratitude journal and use it daily.
  20. Recognize your unique gifts and talents and use them to benefit yourself and others.
  21. Do something creative to wake up your soul.
  22. Slow down and connect with people on a spiritual level.
  23. Fill your cup so you can fill others.   
  24. Exercise your spiritual muscles; forgive, accept and love.
  25. Practice agape love, the purest form of love we can give to ourselves and others.  It is whole, devoid of ego and has no expectations.

TFL Tips

There is strong evidence that having an active spiritual life and knowing that you are part of something greater than yourself has a positive effect on your health and Ideal Being.  Make an effort to continue to feed your soul and to grow spiritually, you will feel the benefits of time well spent.

Regardless of your religious affiliation, let love be your guiding light.  Spirituality is an intrinsic part of who you are as a human being—it is your soul.  Improving the health of your spirit starts with love and compassion for yourself and others, waking up to your life's purpose and energetically connecting with others in order to make the world a better place.
