Definition of Habits:

"An acquired mode of behavior that has become nearly or completely involuntary." - https://www.merriam-webster.com/dictionary/habit

"The action or specific pattern of behavior triggered by the emotional and physical influence of a desired state." - Terrie Elizabeth Reeves, Founder, Empower Health America

"Habits are the learning of a sequence of acts that have become automatic responses to specific situations, which may be functional in order to satisfy specific needs, or to obtain certain goals." - Aarts, H., Paulussen, T., & Schaalma, H., Psychologists

TFL Self-Guided Exercises:

Take time to think about the following questions and perhaps answer them in a personal Ideal Being Journal that you can go back and reference and add to over time. We encourage you to share and discuss your answers with a coach, counselor or friend. You will be asked to answer the same seven questions for each dimension and we encourage you to take the time to do this self-reflective exercise. Your answers will give you greater clarity and a good foundation in which to begin your journey to a state of Ideal Being.

  1. What does your life currently look and feel like in this dimension?
  2. Specifically what are the things you are doing to feel satisfied in this dimension of your life?
  3. What do you envision your life to look and feel like if you gain greater satisfaction in this dimension?
  4. Specifically what would you like to integrate into your life in this dimension?
  5. What are the barriers that are keeping you from improving this dimension of your life?
  6. What can you do to eliminate or navigate around these barriers?
  7. What tools and support do you need to be successful?

Self-awareness Exercise:

Start by determining one habit that you would like to continue and one habit you’d like to change.  Use a note book or journal and write on the outside the word “Aware.”  Write down the habit you want to continue on the first page and 21 pages later write the habit you want to change.  

There is no need to change your behavior.  All you have to do is exactly what you have been doing with one small exception.  You are encouraged to record in your journal each day how the habit is impacting your life and how it makes you feel.  Try and spend five minutes each day recording your feelings.  

After 21 days, review what you have written.  Make sure that you are rested and have the time to truly hear and feel your own words.  Take the time to recognize the positive impact of your healthy behavior and be open to the possibilities of changing the unhealthy behavior.  You will know if you are ready to make the change.  

The way you live your life is up to you.  You are the only one who has the power to change the course of your life one choice at a time.

TFL Actions to Consider:

  1. Evaluate the habits in your life and determine which ones have a positive impact on your life and which ones have a negative impact.
  2. Reflect on you unhealthy habits and trace them back to where they began.  Do the same with your healthy habits.
  3. Formulate an action plan to work on eliminating the unhealthy habits and a plan to reinforce the healthy ones. 
  4. Spend one week journaling about your unhealthy habits and what triggers them in order to raise awareness.
  5. Try to slow down and feel the emotions you are experiencing when you are triggered to behave in an unhealthy way.
  6. Journal how you are feeling i.e. anxious, sad, angry, fearful, loneliness, boredom, etc.
  7. Proactively determine a healthier plan of action to respond to your triggers rather than defaulting to your habitual way of coping.  For example, call a friend, meditate and breathe deeply, take a walk to clear your head, cuddle a child or pet or exercise.
  8. Look for ways to minimize or eliminate the stressors that trigger your unhealthy habits.
  9. Ask for help and surround yourself with people that will encourage and support you to overcome your unhealthy habits.
  10. Find someone to help hold you accountable.
  11. Visualize yourself succeeding and what your life would look like if you were free of your unhealthy habits.
  12. Prepare for the setbacks but don’t spend too much time dwelling on them when they occur.  Acknowledge them and move on.
  13. Train yourself to think differently about your unhealthy habits.  You are not your habits, so don’t let them define you.
  14. Pick a new healthy habit and try to replace your unhealthy habit with it.  For example, exercise, paint, yoga, volunteer or socialize with friends. 
  15. Seek professional help if your unhealthy habits become chronic and they have a prolonged negative impact on your health and/or the health of others.

TFL Tips

Habits can be healthy and unhealthy, so slow down and take inventory.  Reinforce your good habits by acknowledging the positive impact they are having on your life and conversely acknowledge the negative impact your unhealthy habits are having.  Habits are hard to break so if you can’t change your behavior on your own, you are not alone.  Don’t be afraid or embarrassed to ask for help.  It takes more courage to ask for help than to suffer in silence.  Take a leap of faith and ask someone to help you.
