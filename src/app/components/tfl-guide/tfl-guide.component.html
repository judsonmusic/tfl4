Going to the doctor, eating well, exercising and getting proper sleep are all important aspects of well-being, but we hope the 15 dimensions exposed you to the many other factors that impact and influence your overall health and happiness.

What is Ideal Being? To be healthy, happy and whole by reaching a state of deep satisfaction in the dimensions of your life that are important to you.

What defines ideal health, happiness and life satisfaction is personal and different for everyone. Keeping your life balanced and feeling healthy, happy and whole requires you to stay awake, so make it a practice to pause and take inventory in the following dimensions on a regular basis: Nutrition, Activity, Emotion, Habits, Relationships, Health, Financial, Play, Home, Intellect, Self-image, Occupation, Spiritual, Life Balance and Relax.

    HOW CAN I REACH MY IDEAL STATE OF BEING?

We developed a 4-step Train For Life process. Each step is important and the process is fluid, so you may move back and forth between each stage. Remember that you deserve to be deeply satisfied in all the dimensions of your life that are important to you.

The good news is you have already completed steps one and two.

  1. You took the assessment to become more Aware of your life in 15 dimensions and to identify the dimensions in which you are thriving and where you are unsatisfied.
  2. You answered questions to become more Awake to what is important to you and where you are motivated to take action.
  3. Now that you know where you want to take action, become Empowered to use this guide and any additional resources to reach your goals.
  4. Intrinsic motivation is the desire to take action because something is internally and deeply important to you. Become Transformed as you integrate new behaviors and lifestyle choices into your life.

    MOTIVATED TO TAKE ACTION? HOW DO I GET STARTED?

Set intentional and realistic goals: Rome wasn’t built in a day. Similarly, changing behavior requires focused time and energy, so take action in an intentional manner. Focus on what is most important to you. Write your goals down, establish a clear game plan to reach them, and determine a time-line that is realistic. It is important not to get discouraged if you don’t see change right away. Healthy habits that last often take the most time and effort to develop.

"Remember the 21/90 rule: it takes at least 21 days for new or changed behavior to become a habit, and 90 days for it to become a lifestyle." - Dr. Maxwell Maltz, Psycho Cybernetic

Envision success and then create it! Visualize the end result and what your ideal state looks and feels like. Keep positive thoughts in your mind with the end game in sight. Creating a personal contract with yourself will help you stay on course. You can create your own one page contract by following the below template.

  • Type at the top of the document: My Personal Empowerment Contract
  • Time-line: Establish a time-line
  • Goal: What you want to change and why you want to change (be sure the motivation to change is for you and not for someone else)
  • Future state: What your life will look like and feel like once you reach your goal
  • Accountability: Who can help you and what does that look like
  • Relapse: What’s your plan if you relapse
  • Type at the bottom of the document: I can do this because my life is important. (sign your name)

"You cannot create in your life what you cannot envision in your mind." - Terrie Elizabeth Reeves

Track your progress: Let your Ideal Being Score and your Stress Gauge Number serve as a benchmark of your current level of satisfaction and stress you experience in each dimension. Knowing where you are is an important step in determining where you want to go. We encourage you to work your action plans and stay focused. Pause along the way to recognize your achievements and be sure to celebrate your successes. We recommend you do a personal check-up in each dimension every 3 months so you can monitor how you are doing. This will allow you to compare how you are feeling against your original Ideal Being Score and Stress Gauge Number. Nothing stays the same, so stay aware and awake to how life changes impact what is important to you and adjust your course as needed.

"Be patient with yourself. Self-growth is tender; it’s holy ground. There is no greater investment." - Stephen Covey

Stay informed and empowered: Use the action plans in this guide to help you stay inspired. We also encourage you to share your guide if you are working with a counselor, coach or trusted advisor. Friends and family can also be a big help, so seek out people who will provide you the support you need to be successful.

Pause to celebrate your accomplishments: Reassessing where you are each week is an important part of the journey. Take time to appreciate the progress you have made along the way and don’t underestimate the importance of recognizing your hard work. This can be done through positive self-affirmation while meditating, through prayer, gratitude journaling or talking through your accomplishments with a friend or family member. These small pauses will keep you on track. Remember, every intentional action brings you one step closer to your desired state of Ideal Being.

"There is power in the pause, so slow down to recognize your achievements and be grateful." - Terrie Elizabeth Reeves

Rebound when you relapse: Everyone encounters barriers and challenges while making life changes. This is a normal part of the change process, so in your journey toward transformation, don’t focus on the barriers you encounter; just concentrate on the solutions. It is a good idea to establish a recovery plan so when you hit barriers, you have a proactive strategy for dealing with them. For example, if you are trying to lose weight and you stumble on your journey and binge eat for two days, don’t beat yourself up or get discouraged. Pause, take a deep breath, hit the reset button and focus on your accomplishments. Ask for support and recognize that setbacks are a normal part on the road to change.

"Failure is where growth happens, so look at it as a beautiful part of the process; it means you are one step closer to Ideal Being." - Terrie Elizabeth Reeves
