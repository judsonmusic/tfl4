HOW CAN I REACH MY IDEAL STATE OF BEING?

We have developed a 4-step Train For Life process, which will help you reach your ideal state of being. Each stage is an important step in the process.

In working through the TFL process you will become:

  • Aware of a broader way of thinking about your overall health and Ideal Being. Taking the assessment was the first step in this process.
  • Awake to the dimensions in your life that are important to you and where you are motivated to take action.
  • Empowered to take action by working the plans in the dimensions that are important to you.
  • Transformed as new behaviors and lifestyle choices become integrated and a part of your life.

This 4-part TFL process is fluid, so you may move back and forth between each stage; but we encourage you to stay awake to the things that make you happy and to remember that you deserve to be deeply satisfied in all dimensions of your life.

I’M MOTIVATED TO TAKE ACTION! HOW DO I GET STARTED?

  • Set intentional and realistic goals: Rome wasn’t built in a day; similarly, changing behavior requires focused energy and time. Don’t get discouraged if you don’t see change right away. Healthy habits that last often take the most time and effort to develop.
    "Remember the 21/90 rule: it takes at least 21 days for new or changed behavior to become a habit, and 90 days for it to become a lifestyle.” Dr. Maxwell Maltz, “Psycho Cybernetics: A New Way to Get More Living Out of Life”
  • Envision your success and then create it: Visualize the end result and what your ideal state looks and feels like. Keep positive thoughts in your mind, the endgame in sight and know your goals can become a reality. Create a personal contract with yourself to help you stay motivated.
    "You cannot create in your life what you cannot envision in your mind." Terrie Elizabeth Reeves, Founder, Empower Health America
  • Follow your progress: This guide provides you an Ideal Being Score in each dimension and an overall Ideal Being Score that benchmarks your current level of satisfaction. Knowing where you are is an important step to determining where you want to go. We encourage you to utilize your action plans and to take breaks along the way to celebrate your successes. To see how you’re doing, we welcome you to print this guide out as a reference tool and to retake the TFL assessment every three months by logging back in to the TFL Assessment site, and taking the assessment again. This will allow you to compare your updated results to your original ones.
  • Stay informed and want to go. We encourage you to empowered: Your Empowerment Guide includes action plans in each dimension designed to help you stay empowered and on course, so we encourage you to use this information by putting it into action. Sharing your guide and working with a counselor, coach or trusted advisor on your action plans and goals is another way to help you along your path to greater happiness and satisfaction. Friends and family can also be a big help, so seek out people who will provide you support and encouragement.
  • Pause to celebrate your accomplishments: Reassessing where you are is an important part of the journey. Take time to appreciate the progress you have made along the way and don’t underestimate the importance of recognizing your hard work. This can be done through positive self-affirmation while meditating, through prayer of appreciation, gratitude journaling or talking through your accomplishments with a friend or family member. These small pauses will help to keep you empowered and on track. Remember, each intentional action brings you one step closer to meeting your goals, and ultimately to your desired state of Ideal Being.
    "There is power in the pause, so slow down to recognize your achievements and be grateful." Terrie Elizabeth Reeves
  • Rebound when you relapse: Everyone encounters barriers and challenges when setting goals around life changes. This is a normal part of the change process, so when you journey toward transformation, don’t focus on the barriers you encounter; just concentrate on the solutions. It is a good idea to establish a recovery plan so when you hit barriers you have a proactive strategy for dealing with them. For example, if you are trying to lose weight and you go off the reservation and binge eat for two days, don’t beat yourself up or get discouraged or stuck. Pause, take a deep breath and recognize the successes you have made, hit the restart button, ask for help and recognize that setbacks are part of the road to change.
    "Failure is where growth happens, so look at it as a beautiful part of the process; it means you are one step closer to Ideal Being." Terrie Elizabeth Reeves
